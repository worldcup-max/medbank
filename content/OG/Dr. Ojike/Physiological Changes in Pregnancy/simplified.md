# Physiological Changes in Pregnancy — Simplified

**One line:** every organ system is remodelled to feed the fetus, protect the mother from bleeding at delivery, and keep the placenta perfused — and none of it is disease.

---

## The three purposes (memorise these — everything else follows)

1. **Support the metabolic demands** of the growing fetus and placenta
2. **Protect the mother** from the stressors of pregnancy and parturition — **especially haemorrhage at delivery**
3. **Ensure adequate utero-placental perfusion** for nutrient and gas exchange

**Driven by:** the **corpus luteum** and the **developing placenta** — via **oestrogen, progesterone and hCG**

> These changes are **reversible** and **not pathological**.

---

# CARDIOVASCULAR

## The blood volume numbers

| | Increase |
|---|---|
| **Plasma volume** | **40–50%** |
| **RBC mass** | only **15–20%** (via erythropoietin) |

**Plasma runs far ahead of red cells** → **haemodilution** → **PHYSIOLOGICAL ANAEMIA OF PREGNANCY**

> **Clinical importance:** in pregnancy, **Hb of 10.5–11 g/dL is NORMAL.** Do not treat it as anaemia.

---

## Cardiac output

**CO increases 30–50%**

**CO = HR × SV**

- **Stroke volume ↑ 20–30%** — **early**, from increased end-diastolic volume and myocardial hypertrophy
- **Heart rate ↑ 15–20 bpm** — **progressively**, throughout pregnancy

---

## Systemic vascular resistance

**SVR ↓ 25–30%**, from three things:

1. **Progesterone** relaxes smooth muscle
2. **Local vasodilators** — **nitric oxide (NO)** and **prostacyclin (PGI₂)**
3. **A low-resistance, high-flow utero-placental circuit** is created

---

## Blood pressure — the apparent contradiction

Cardiac output goes **up**, yet blood pressure goes **DOWN**.

> **Why?** Because the drop in SVR is **massive** and outweighs the rise in output.

- **Diastolic drops more than systolic**
- **Lowest BP is at 20–24 weeks**, then gradually returns to baseline at term

> **N.B. This is exactly why 20 weeks is the cut-off for gestational hypertension (PIH).** Before 20 weeks the BP should be falling — so hypertension then is probably pre-existing.

---

## Supine hypotensive syndrome

**Lying supine** → gravid uterus compresses the **IVC and lower aorta**

↓ venous return → ↓ stroke volume → ↓ cardiac output

⇓

**SUPINE HYPOTENSIVE SYNDROME** — **dizziness, pallor, placental hypoperfusion**

> **Always nurse late-pregnancy women in LEFT LATERAL TILT**, to displace the uterus off the great vessels.

---

# RESPIRATORY

**Purpose:** meet the **20% increase in oxygen demand**, and clear fetal CO₂.

| Parameter | Change |
|---|---|
| **Tidal volume** | **↑ 30–40%** |
| **Minute ventilation** (TV × RR) | **↑ 30–40%** |
| **Functional residual capacity** | **↓ 10–20%** |
| **Residual volume** | **↓ 15–20%** |
| **Vital capacity** | **UNCHANGED** |

> Pattern: **volumes going IN go up · volumes left behind go down · vital capacity doesn't move.**

---

# RENAL

**Job:** process both maternal waste **and** fetal metabolic byproducts.

## Anatomical changes

**Hydronephrosis and hydroureters**, from:

1. **Progesterone-induced smooth muscle relaxation**
2. **Compression of ureters at the pelvic brim** by the expanding uterus

**Worse on the RIGHT.** Why?

- **Dextrorotation of the uterus** (it twists to the right)
- The **left ureter is cushioned by the sigmoid colon**

> **N.B.** Stasis of urine → increased risk of progression from **asymptomatic bacteriuria** to **acute pyelonephritis**.

---

## Renal haemodynamics

- **Renal plasma flow ↑ up to 50–80%**
- **GFR ↑ up to 50%**

**Hyperfiltration** ⇒ clearance of **creatinine, urea and uric acid** is highly elevated

⇒ **serum creatinine FALLS to 0.4–0.6 mg/dL** (non-pregnant: 0.8)

---

> **THE HIGH-YIELD POINT:** a creatinine of **0.8 mg/dL or higher in pregnancy** — which would be **normal** outside pregnancy — **warrants evaluation for renal impairment or pre-eclampsia.**

---

**Also from the high GFR:**

- Renal threshold for glucose reabsorption is exceeded → **GLYCOSURIA** (normal in pregnancy)
- **Mild increase in urinary protein excretion**

---

# GASTROINTESTINAL

> **Primarily FUNCTIONAL changes, driven by the smooth muscle relaxant effect of PROGESTERONE.**

**High progesterone causes:**

- **↓ lower oesophageal sphincter (LES) tone** → **GERD**
  (LES tone down **+** intra-abdominal pressure up from the expanding uterus)
- **↓ intestinal motility** → **constipation**
- **↓ gall bladder motility** → **cholelithiasis**

**Oestrogen causes:**

- **Gingival hyperplasia and bleeding gums — epulis gravidarum**

**Also:**

- **Haemorrhoids** — from constipation **plus** raised pelvic venous pressure from uterine compression of the vena cava
- **ALP doubles or triples** — from **placental** production (so a high ALP in pregnancy is not liver disease)

---

# HAEMATOLOGICAL

> **Pregnancy is a HYPERCOAGULABLE state** — an evolutionary adaptation to prevent fatal postpartum haemorrhage, **but** it carries significant VTE risk.

**Three changes, all pushing the same way:**

- **↑ procoagulant factors** — **fibrinogen, VII, VIII, IX, XII**, and **von Willebrand factor**
- **↓ anticoagulant protein S**
- **↓ fibrinolysis**

⇒ **Risk of DVT and PE increased 4–5 fold**

Also: **leucocytosis**

---

# ENDOCRINE

> The endocrine milieu **completely revolutionises** to prioritise a continuous supply of **glucose and amino acids** to the fetus.

**↑ Oestrogen · human placental lactogen · progesterone · cortisol · placental growth hormone**

⇓

**Hormone-induced INSULIN RESISTANCE**

⇒ the mother must **increase insulin secretion** to stay euglycaemic

---

> **N.B. GESTATIONAL DIABETES occurs when the maternal pancreatic response is insufficient to overcome the hormone-induced insulin resistance.**

---

## Pituitary

- **Enlarges up to 135%** — from **hypertrophy and hyperplasia of LACTOTROPHS** (prolactin-producing cells), **stimulated by oestrogen**

⇓

- **Prone to SHEEHAN SYNDROME** if severe **postpartum haemorrhage and hypovolaemic shock** occur

> An enlarged gland outgrows its blood supply — so a sudden drop in perfusion infarcts it.

---

# SKIN & MUSCULOSKELETAL

**Hyperpigmentation** — from **↑ MSH (melanocyte stimulating hormone)** and **oestrogen**

Darkening of:

- **Areolae**
- **Perineum**
- **Axillae**
- **Midline of abdomen — LINEA NIGRA**

**Also:**

- **Melasma (chloasma)**
- **Striae gravidarum**
- **Vascular changes** — **palmar erythema, spider angiomas**
- **Progressive lumbar lordosis**
- **Joint laxity**

---
