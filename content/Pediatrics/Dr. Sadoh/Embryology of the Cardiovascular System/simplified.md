# Embryology of the CVS — the simple version

## The one theme that runs through everything

> Every congenital heart defect is just **one embryology step gone wrong.**

So this whole lecture is really a list of **building steps** — and for each step, the defect you get if it fails. Learn the steps in order, and the defects fall out on their own.

And one cell type keeps showing up as the villain:

> **Neural crest cells** → they build the **outflow tract**. When they fail, you get the **big cyanotic defects: TGA, TOF, Truncus arteriosus.**

Circle "neural crest." It's the answer to half the exam.

---

## Why the heart forms FIRST (day 18)

The tiny embryo feeds by **diffusion**. Around **week 3** it gets too big — the inner cells starve. So it builds a **delivery system first**.

> **CVS = the earliest functional system. Day 18 → 12 weeks.**

---

## The building steps, in order

Think of it as **7 steps**: **tube → loop → converge/wedge → veins → atrial septum → ventricular septum → outflow (spiral) + arches.**

---

### Step 1 — Make a tube

Two cords (from **splanchnic mesoderm**) fuse into **one straight heart tube** (week 4).

It's anchored top and bottom, and already labelled:

> **Bottom = venous end = future ATRIA. Top = arterial end = future VENTRICLES.**

That looks backwards (veins low, arteries high) — but the next step fixes it.

**The tube's regions, top to bottom:**
Truncus arteriosus → Bulbus cordis → Primitive ventricle → Primitive atrium → **Sinus venosus** (the receiving end, where all veins drain).

---

### Step 2 — LOOP it

The tube grows but is pinned at both ends with nowhere to go — so it **coils**, like a hose you push from both ends.

> **Looping flips the layout:** atria end up **on top and behind**, ventricles **below and in front** — the adult arrangement.

**The defect if it loops the wrong way:**

> **Wrong-direction looping → DEXTROCARDIA** (heart on the wrong side).

---

### Step 3 — CONVERGE and WEDGE (line things up)

After looping, the inlets and outlets aren't over the right ventricles yet. Two little movements align them:

- **Convergence** — brings inlet and outlet into the same plane
- **Wedging** — AV canal shifts **right**, bulbus shifts **left**, so each valve sits over its own ventricle

**Defects if alignment fails:**

> **Wedging fails → Double Outlet RV** (both great vessels over the RV)
> **Convergence fails → Double Inlet LV** (both AV valves into the LV)

---

### Step 4 — The veins (3 pairs, learn their adult fates)

Three vein pairs drain into the sinus venosus:

| Vein | Carries | Becomes |
|---|---|---|
| **Vitelline** | blood from **yolk sac** | **hepatic + portal veins** |
| **Umbilical** | **oxygenated** blood from **placenta** | **ductus venosus** → ligamentum venosum |
| **Cardinal** | body blood | **SVC and IVC** |

> **Weird fact:** the **umbilical vein carries OXYGENATED blood** — because in the fetus, **the placenta is the lung.**

**The big theme here:**

> **Everything systemic is RIGHT-sided.** SVC = **right** anterior cardinal vein. Left side mostly disappears. That's why you have one SVC and one IVC, both on the right.

**Defect:** left anterior cardinal fails to regress → **persistent left SVC** (drains into the coronary sinus; usually harmless — commonest thoracic venous anomaly).

---

### Step 5 — Split the ATRIA (but leave a hole)

The fetus needs blood to **skip the lungs**, so the atria are divided **but kept connected** by the **foramen ovale**.

**Two walls grow down, and a hole is always kept open:**

1. **Septum primum** grows down → its gap is **foramen primum**
2. Before that closes, holes appear up top → **foramen secundum** (flow never stops)
3. **Septum secundum** (thicker, muscular) grows down beside it but **stops short** → leaves the **foramen ovale**

> **Septum primum = the thin flap. Septum secundum = the thick wall with the foramen ovale gap.**

**The foramen ovale is a one-way valve:**

- **Before birth:** right pressure high → flap pushed **open** → blood goes **right → left** (skips lungs) ✓
- **At birth:** first breath → left pressure rises → flap pushed **shut** → fuses → becomes the **fossa ovalis**

**Defects (learn the pairing):**

| Defect | Cause |
|---|---|
| **ASD secundum** (commonest ASD) | foramen ovale too big / too much resorption |
| **ASD primum** | septum primum fails to meet the **endocardial cushions** |
| **Patent foramen ovale** (25% of adults) | septa never fuse |

> **Endocardial cushions** also build the AV valves. Fail them → **AVSD → classic in DOWN SYNDROME.**

---

### Step 6 — Split the VENTRICLES (from FOUR pieces)

The ventricular wall grows **UP from the floor**, and it's built from **4 parts fusing**: inlet, outlet, muscular, **membranous**.

> **4 parts = 4 chances to go wrong = why VSD is the COMMONEST congenital heart defect.**

**The membranous part is last and smallest** → most likely to fail:

> **Perimembranous VSD = ~80% of all VSDs.**

---

### Step 7 — Split the OUTFLOW (the spiral) + build the arches

The heart has 2 ventricles but **1 outflow pipe** (bulbus cordis + truncus). A wall must split it into **aorta + pulmonary trunk** — and it grows in a **SPIRAL** (which is why the two vessels wrap around each other).

**This is neural-crest territory. Learn these 3 defects:**

| If... | You get |
|---|---|
| ridges don't fuse at all (neural crest fails) | **Truncus arteriosus** (one common trunk) |
| division is **straight, not spiral** | **TGA** (aorta off RV, pulmonary off LV) |
| division is **unequal** | **Tetralogy of Fallot** (PS + overriding aorta + VSD + RVH) |

> **Straight instead of spiral = TGA.** That single line is worth memorising.

---

### The aortic arches — the "keep some, demolish most" scaffold

Six pairs of arches form, then most are demolished. Learn only the survivors:

- **3rd → carotids**
- **4th → aortic arch** (left) / right subclavian (right)
- **5th → nothing**
- **6th → pulmonary arteries + DUCTUS ARTERIOSUS** (left)

> **Memory:** **3 = carotid, 4 = aortic arch, 6 = pulmonary + ductus.** (5 does nothing.)

**Defects:**

> **Ductus (6th arch) fails to close → PDA.**
> **Right 4th arch persists → right-sided aortic arch.**

---

## The master table — step → defect

| Step | If it fails |
|---|---|
| Looping | **Dextrocardia** |
| Wedging | **DORV** |
| Convergence | **DILV** |
| Atrial septation | **ASD** (± AVSD via cushions → Down's) |
| Ventricular septation | **VSD** (commonest CHD) |
| Spiral outflow (neural crest) | **TGA, TOF, Truncus** |
| 6th arch closure | **PDA** |

---

## Putting it all together

The heart is the **first system** to form (day 18) because the embryo outgrew diffusion.

It builds in order: **tube → loop → wedge → veins → atrial septum → ventricular septum → spiral outflow + arches.**

**Every defect is one step failing:** wrong loop = **dextrocardia**, bad wedging = **DORV**, atrial failure = **ASD** (cushions → **AVSD/Down's**), four-part ventricular wall failing = **VSD** (the commonest), and **neural-crest / spiral outflow** failures = the big cyanotic three — **TGA, TOF, Truncus**. The **6th arch ductus** not closing = **PDA**.

Two things carry the most marks: **neural crest → outflow tract defects**, and **straight-instead-of-spiral = TGA.**
