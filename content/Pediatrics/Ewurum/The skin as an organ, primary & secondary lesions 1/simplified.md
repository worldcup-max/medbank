# The Skin as an Organ — the simple version

## Start with one idea

> The skin is not a wrapper. It is an **organ** — the largest one you have.

An organ does jobs. So the whole lecture is really answering: **what jobs does it do, what is it made of, and what does it look like when it goes wrong?**

Three questions. Three sections. That is the entire lecture.

---

## Job list — 4 things the skin does

Think of the skin as a **house wall**:

**1. It keeps things in and things out** — water, salt and heat stay in; germs and toxins stay out.

**2. It has plumbing** — two glands:

- **Sebaceous** → **sebum** → **oil** → lubricates
- **Sweat** → **sweat** → **water** → cools you as it evaporates

**3. It is wired** — nerve fibres under the skin sense **pain, temperature, pressure, touch**.

**4. It is the thermostat** — thermoregulation.

> Memory hook: **P.S.S.T.** — **P**rotection, **S**ecretion, **S**ensation, **T**hermoregulation.

---

## What it is made of — 3 layers

From the outside going in:

**1. Epidermis** — thin, cellular, **no blood vessels at all**

**2. Dermis (corium)** — thick, fibrous, **full of blood vessels**

**3. Hypodermis (subcutaneous)** — **fat**

---

### The one fact that ties this together

> The epidermis has **NO blood vessels**.

So how does it eat?

**It borrows from the dermis below it.** The dermis has the capillaries, so the dermis feeds the epidermis.

That is why the dermis' *main function* is written as **"to provide physical support and nutrients to the epidermis."**

---

### Inside the epidermis — 5 sub-layers

Read them **top to bottom**, and follow the life story of one cell:

| Layer | What is happening |
|---|---|
| **Stratum corneum** | Cell is **dead and hard** — this is the armour |
| **Granular layer** | Cell is **dying** — the transition. Keratohyalin + lamellar granules |
| **Spinous layer** | Cell is **making keratin** — the net |
| **Basal layer** | Cell is **born** — and this is where **melanocytes** live |
| **Basement membrane** | The **floor** — joins and separates epidermis from dermis |

> So a skin cell is **born at the bottom** and **dies at the top**. It gets pushed up as new cells are made below it.

---

### The melanin fact — this is a favourite exam question

> **Everybody has the SAME number of melanocytes.**

Black skin and white skin have **equal numbers** of melanocytes.

The difference is **how much melanin those cells make**.

---

### Inside the dermis — 2 sub-layers

- **Papillary** — the **upper, thinner** one. **Small** vessels. Feeds the epidermis just above it.
- **Reticular** — the **lower, thicker** one. **Large** vessels, dense collagen in bundles.

Five things live in the reticular layer:

- **Fibroblasts** — the **repairmen**
- **Mast cells** — the **soldiers**
- **Lymphatics** — the **drainage and defence**
- **Rete pegs** — the **velcro** that stops epidermis sliding off dermis
- **Ground substance** — the **gel** that fills the gaps

---

## When it goes wrong — lesions

A **lesion** is just: *skin that looks different from the skin next to it.*

Different in **colour, shape, size or texture**.

---

### The one distinction everything hangs on

> **PRIMARY** = it appeared **on healthy skin**. It is the **first** thing that happened.
>
> **SECONDARY** = it appeared **on top of a primary lesion**. Something happened *to* the first lesion — you scratched it, it was treated, or it progressed.

Ask yourself: *could this have been the very first change?*

- Yes → primary
- No, something must have come before it → secondary

---

### Primary lesions — sort them by 2 questions

**Question 1: Can I feel it?**

- **NO, it's flat** → **macule** (< 1 cm) or **patch** (> 1 cm)

**Question 2: If I can feel it, what is inside?**

| Inside | Small (< 1 cm) | Big (> 1 cm) |
|---|---|---|
| **Clear fluid** | **Vesicle** | **Bulla** |
| **Pus** | **Pustule** | — |
| **Solid** | **Papule** (< 0.5 cm) / **Nodule** (≤ 1 cm) | **Plaque** (raised & flat-topped) |

Two odd ones out:

- **Wheal** — solid-feeling but it is **swelling (oedema)**, and it **comes and goes** (evanescent)
- **Burrow** — a **tunnel**, with a **parasite living in it** (scabies)

---

### The size numbers — learn these, they are marks

- **0.5 cm** → the papule line
- **1 cm** → the line for **everything else** (vesicle/bulla, macule/patch, nodule)

---

### Secondary lesions — group them by what happened

**Something dried on top:**

- **Crust** — dried blood/serum/pus stuck to the surface

**Something came off:**

- **Scaling** — flakes of stratum corneum
- **Desquamation** — **sheets** peeling (burns, drug reaction)
- **Erosion** — a blister whose **roof was removed**; moist
- **Excoriation** — **you scratched the epidermis clean off**; red dermis showing
- **Ulcer** — an **open sore**, goes deeper

**Something split:**

- **Fissure** — a **crack**, wedge-shaped, narrows at the base

**Something thickened:**

- **Lichenification** — **rubbing** made the skin thick and leathery

**Something died:**

- **Scar** — fibrous tissue **replacing** normal tissue
- **Gangrene** — **tissue death** from **loss of blood supply**

---

## Putting it all together

The skin is an **organ** with **four jobs** and **three layers**.

The **epidermis** is dead armour that has **no blood supply** — so the **dermis feeds it**.

Melanocyte **number** is the same in everyone; melanin **amount** is not.

When skin changes, ask **one** question: *was this first, or did something come before it?* That single question splits every lesion into **primary** or **secondary**.

Then for primary lesions, ask **flat or raised**, and if raised, **what is inside** — and the name falls out on its own.
