# Fungal Skin Infections — Simplified

**One line:** fungal spores thrive in our climate; crowding and poor hygiene spread them; and they attack skin, hair, nails and mucosa.

---

## Why fungal infections are common here

- Spores occur in **hot humid or arid tropical** environments
- **Poor personal and community hygiene**
- **Closeness among large families**
- **Institutions** — schools, camps, barracks

---

## The classification — memorise this shape first

| **SUPERFICIAL** (5) | **DEEP** (5) |
|---|---|
| Candidiasis | Mycetoma (Madura foot) |
| Tinea capitis | Blastomycosis |
| Tinea corporis | Coccidiomycosis |
| Tinea pedis | Histoplasmosis |
| Pityriasis versicolor | Sporotrichosis |

> **Superficial = skin, hair, nails, mucosa.** Diagnosed by **KOH scraping**, treated with **topical or oral antifungals**.

> **Deep = lungs, bone, viscera.** Mostly **inhaled**, spread by **blood**, treated with **amphotericin B** or oral azoles.

---

## CANDIDIASIS

**Organisms:** *Candida albicans* (most common), *C. tropicalis*, *C. stellatoidea*, *C. krusei*

**Sites:** skin, mucous membranes, nail folds and nails, GIT, genital tract — and **viscera** in systemic infection

**Skin lesions:** dusky red **macerated or erosive** lesions in skin folds of the **neck, flanks, large flexures and limbs**. Scratching → fissuring and bleeding → **secondary bacterial infection**

### Oral thrush

**Curdy or whitish-grey, friable, cheesy pseudomembranous patches** on **markedly reddened mucosa**

Sites: lips, buccal mucosa, tongue, palate — extending to oesophagus/pharynx

> **The diagnostic trick:** scrape the plaque off and you get **mild punctate bleeding**. That confirms it.

**Predisposing:** diabetes mellitus · retroviral disease · **prolonged antibiotic and corticosteroid use** · leukaemia

**Treat:** clotrimazole · nystatin · amphotericin B · miconazole gel · proper oral hygiene

---

## TINEA CAPITIS — scalp

**Organism:** ***Trichophyton tonsurans*** most often; occasionally *Microsporum canis*

**Lesions:** erythematous scaly **circular plaques (ringworm)**; hairs become **brittle and broken at different lengths**

> Multiple sites → **moth-eaten appearance** of the hair

Plus: confluent patches of **alopecia**, **severe pruritus**

### Two special forms

**KERION** — the host's own **cellular immune response** creates an **inflammatory, boggy mass** with crusts, pus-filled bumps and localised hair loss

**FAVUS** — severe chronic form, ***Trichophyton schoenleinii***

- Thick, yellowish, **cup-shaped crusts called scutula** around follicles
- **Mousy odour**
- "**Honey-combed**" scalp lesions
- Untreated → destroys follicles → matting → **permanent scarring alopecia**

**Diagnose:** scraping + **KOH**

**Treat:** **oral** griseofulvin, itraconazole or terbinafine + topical cream/shampoo

> Note: tinea capitis needs **ORAL** treatment. Topical alone cannot reach the fungus inside the hair shaft.

---

## TINEA CORPORIS — body

**Glabrous skin** (smooth, hairless) — **excluding palms, soles and groin**

**Organisms:** *T. rubrum*, *T. mentagrophytes*, *Microsporum canis*

**The classic lesion:**

> Dry, mildly erythematous, elevated, scaly papule/plaque that **spreads centrifugally and clears centrally** → the **annular (ring) lesion**, with an **active edge showing vesicles and pustules**

**Caught from:** infected people, surfaces, and **pets** (*M. canis*)

### Named by site

- **Tinea faciale** — face
- **Tinea axillaris** — axilla
- **Tinea cruris** — groin
- **Tinea manuum** — hands
- **Tinea unguium** — nails

**Diagnose:** clinical · skin scraping + KOH · **culture** to identify species

**Treat:** topical **imidazoles, terbinafine, naftifine**; severe → oral **griseofulvin, itraconazole**

---

## TINEA PEDIS — athlete's foot

**Toe webs and soles**

**Organisms:** *T. rubrum* · *T. mentagrophytes* · ***Epidermophyton floccosum***

**Features:**

- **Subdigital crevices fissured**, with maceration and peeling
- Less commonly: chronic diffuse **hyperkeratosis of the sole**, mild erythema
- Occasionally vesicles and bullae → pustular
- **Severe tenderness, itching, persistent foul odour**

**Diagnose:** KOH preparation showing **mycelia**, or culture

**Treat:**

- **Avoid occlusive footwear**; **dry carefully between the toes** after bathing
- Topical **zinc undecylenate powder**, **imidazole cream**, oral **griseofulvin**

> Remember from the bacterial lecture: those fissures are a **classic entry point for cellulitis**. Treating the fungus prevents the bacteria.

---

## PITYRIASIS VERSICOLOR

**Chronic infection of the STRATUM CORNEUM**

**Organism:** the dimorphic yeast ***Malassezia furfur***, found in skin **rich in sebum**

**Who:** adolescents and young adults

**Predisposing:** warm humid environment · excessive sweating · occlusion · **high plasma cortisol** · immunosuppression · malnourishment · genetic susceptibility

**Lesions:** **hypo- OR hyperpigmented macules** with **fine scale**, merging into confluent patches

> That is what "versicolor" means — it varies in colour.

**Sites:** neck, upper chest, back, upper arms — the **sebum-rich** areas

**Diagnose:** **Wood lamp** · KOH · skin biopsy

**Treat:** **selenium sulfide** overnight · imidazole or terbinafine cream twice daily 2–4 weeks · oral **ketoconazole, fluconazole or itraconazole**

# THE DEEP MYCOSES

> **Three of them share one pattern:** inhaled spores → **primary infection in the LUNGS** → **haematogenous spread** → skin lesions.

---

## HISTOPLASMOSIS

***Histoplasma capsulatum*** and ***H. duboisii*** — **inhaled**

Found in **soil and droppings of bats and poultry**

**Lungs first** → spreads to **liver, bone marrow, spleen, lymph nodes, subcutaneous tissue**

**Skin:** papules, pustules, nodules anywhere on the body

- ***H. duboisii*** (**African histoplasmosis**) → **subcutaneous nodules that umbilicate and suppurate or ulcerate**
- ***H. capsulatum*** → **lungs**; productive cough and constitutional symptoms

**Diagnose:** culture from sputum, bone marrow, lymph node biopsy, pus or ulcer scrapings

**Treat:** IV **amphotericin B**; oral **itraconazole or ketoconazole**

---

## SPOROTRICHOSIS — the odd one out

***Sporothrix schenckii***, which **grows on thorny plants**

> **Not inhaled — IMPLANTED.** A thorn prick pushes it into the skin.

**Course:** firm, painless or mildly tender **subcutaneous nodule** at the trauma site → purple/violaceous/brown/dark grey → grows, becomes pustular → opens into a **chronic hollowed-out ulcer**

Spreads by **lymphatic or haematogenous** routes to muscle, bone, joints. Inhaled spores can infect lung.

**Diagnose:** microscopy of scrapings or biopsy

**Treat:** **potassium iodide** · IV amphotericin B · oral itraconazole

---

## BLASTOMYCOSIS

***Blastomyces dermatitidis*** — **inhaled**

**Lungs** → **skin, bone, genital tract**

**Skin:** irregularly crusted plaques, superficial ulcers, or firm subcutaneous nodules on **exposed** parts

**Diagnose:** characteristic **budding cells** on KOH

**Treat:** IV **amphotericin B**

---

## COCCIDIOMYCOSIS

***Coccidioides immitis*** — **inhaled**

**Lungs** → **skin, subcutaneous tissue, bones**

**Skin:** **panniculitis, abscesses and draining sinuses**

**Treat:** oral **ketoconazole or itraconazole**

---

## MYCETOMA (Madura foot)

Chronic infection of **skin, subcutaneous tissue and BONES of the FEET**

- Takes a **very long time** to develop
- **Rare in children**
- Introduced by **minor injuries — a splinter or thorn prick**
- Common in **agricultural workers**

**Two kinds, depending on the organism:**

| | Cause |
|---|---|
| **Eumycetoma** | **Fungi** — *Pseudallescheria boydii*, *Madurella grisea*, *M. mycetomatis* |
| **Actinomycetoma** | **Filamentous bacteria** — *Nocardia brasiliensis*, *Actinomadura madurae*, *Streptomyces somaliensis* |

**Treat:** **itraconazole**

## Quick treatment map

| Condition | First-line |
|---|---|
| Oral thrush | Nystatin / clotrimazole / miconazole gel |
| Tinea capitis | **ORAL** griseofulvin / itraconazole / terbinafine |
| Tinea corporis | Topical imidazole; oral if severe |
| Tinea pedis | Keep dry; topical imidazole / zinc undecylenate |
| Pityriasis versicolor | Selenium sulfide; imidazole cream |
| Histoplasmosis | IV amphotericin B |
| Sporotrichosis | **Potassium iodide** |
| Blastomycosis | IV amphotericin B |
| Coccidiomycosis | Oral ketoconazole / itraconazole |
| Mycetoma | Itraconazole |
