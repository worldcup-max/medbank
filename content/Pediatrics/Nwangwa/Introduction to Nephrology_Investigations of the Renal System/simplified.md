# Introduction to Nephrology / Renal Investigations — Simplified

**One line:** seven categories of investigation, and the trick is knowing which question each one answers.

---

## What nephrology is

The branch of paediatrics for **diagnosis, evaluation and management of disorders of the kidneys and urinary tract in children**.

**A nephrologist must:** evaluate and diagnose · treat · **monitor growth and development** · provide **renal replacement therapy** · **educate and counsel families**

---

# ANATOMY

![Basic anatomy of the kidneys](img/kidney-anatomy.jpg)

**Paired, bean-shaped, RETROPERITONEAL** organs at **T12–L3**

> **The LEFT kidney is more SUPERIOR than the right.**

**Covered by:** renal fascia = **Gerota's fascia**

| | Male | Female |
|---|---|---|
| Weight | **150 g** | **135 g** |

**Size:** 10–12 cm long · 5–7 cm wide · 2–3 cm thick

---

## Blood supply and nerves

> **Receives about 20% of CARDIAC OUTPUT** — from the renal arteries at **L2**

**Sympathetic:** spinal cord **T8–L1** → **vasoconstriction**

**Parasympathetic:** **vagus nerves** → **vasodilation**

---

## The nephron — the functional unit

![The nephron](img/nephron.jpg)

Kidney divided into **cortex** and **medulla**.

1. **Renal corpuscle** — glomerulus + Bowman's capsule
2. **PCT** — in the **cortex**
3. **Descending loop of Henle** — cortex → medulla
4. **Ascending limb**
5. **Distal convoluted tubule** — **cortex**
6. **Collecting duct** — cortex → medulla → renal pelvis

---

# 7 FUNCTIONS OF THE KIDNEY

1. **Filtration and excretion** of metabolic waste
2. **Fluid and electrolyte balance**
3. **Blood pressure regulation**
4. **Endocrine function**
5. **Acid-base homeostasis**
6. **Regulation of red blood cell production**
7. **Calcium and bone metabolism**

> Notice: only the first is "making urine". The other six explain why renal failure causes **anaemia, bone disease, hypertension and acidosis**.

---

# 9 COMMON RENAL DISORDERS

**CAKUT · UTI · Nephrotic syndrome · AGN · AKI · CKD · VUR · Haemolytic uraemic syndrome · Renal tubular disorders**

---

## 7 risk factors

Drug toxicity · family history/genetics · obesity · hypertension · **prematurity** · diabetes · autoimmune disorders

---

## 7 clinical features

**Oliguria/anuria · oedema · haematuria · proteinuria · hypertension · electrolyte imbalance · lower abdominal pain**

---

# INVESTIGATIONS

## Why investigate — 5 reasons

1. **Confirm a clinical diagnosis**
2. Assess **structural and functional integrity**
3. Assess **severity and extent**
4. **Prognostication**
5. **Exclude co-morbidities**

> **And always consider: diagnostic value · availability · COST · sustainability.**

# 1. URINE EXAMINATION

![Urine examination](img/urine-examination.jpeg)

**Non-invasive.** Yield depends on **collection, preparation and testing**.

> **Examine FRESH** — otherwise cells disintegrate and bacteria overgrow. Store at **4 °C** if you must.

---

## 6 collection methods

- **MSU** (with a long bladder time)
- **Clean catch**
- **Bagging** — **high contamination risk**; valued for its **negative predictive value**
- **SPA** — **MOST RELIABLE**, useful **under 2 years**
- **Catheterization**
- Mid-stream urinary collector

---

## Culture thresholds — depends on HOW it was collected

| Method | Significant growth |
|---|---|
| **SPA** | **ANY growth** — bladder urine should be sterile |
| **CSU** | **≥ 10⁴ CFU/mL** |
| **MSU** | **≥ 10⁵ CFU/mL** |

**More detail:**

- **Catheter in a girl / MSU in a circumcised boy** → usually **> 50,000 CFU/mL**; UTI possible at 10,000–50,000
- **MSU in a girl / uncircumcised boy** → **> 100,000 CFU/mL** in a symptomatic patient; **pyuria usually present**
- **Asymptomatic + no pyuria** → probably **COLONIZATION, not infection**

> **Urinary frequency** shortens bladder incubation time → UTI possible at **low colony counts**.

---

## Dipstick — 11 pads

**Leukocyte · nitrite · protein · pH · blood · specific gravity · ketone · bilirubin · glucose · ascorbic acid · urobilinogen**

**How it works:** urine constituents react with chemicals in each pad → **colour change** → intensity read against the chart.

> **Result quality depends on strictly following the manufacturer's instructions.**

---

## Protein grading — learn these

| Dipstick | mg/dL |
|---|---|
| **Trace** | **< 30** |
| **+** | **30** |
| **2+** | **100** |
| **3+** | **300** |
| **4+** | **> 1000** |

---

## False proteinuria results

**FALSE NEGATIVE:** **dilute** urine · **very acidic** urine · proteinuria from other proteins

**FALSE POSITIVE:** **concentrated** urine · **alkaline** urine · **gross haematuria** · **delay in reading** · contamination with vaginal secretions or antiseptic

> **Memory hook: dilute and acidic hide it; concentrated and alkaline fake it.**

---

## Also done

**Macroscopy** (appearance, colour change, odour) · **microscopy** (RBC, WBC, **casts**, crystals) · **culture and sensitivity** · **spot urine protein-creatinine ratio**

# 2. BLOOD TESTS

**Basic:** Se/U/Cr · calcium · phosphate · magnesium · **vitamin D assay** · FBC · MP · ESR · **arterial blood gases**

**Immunologic:**

- **C3, C4, CH50** — complement
- **ASO titre** and **Anti-DNAse B** — post-streptococcal
- **IgG, IgA** — SLE, JRA
- **ANCA** — **Wegener granulomatosis, crescentic glomerulonephritis**
- **Hormonal** — T3, T4, TSH

---

# 3. GFR ESTIMATION

**For:** assessing renal function · detecting disease · severity · prognostication

> **There is NO perfect substance for measuring it.**

**Markers used:** **inulin · polyfructose · Cr-EDTA · creatinine · cystatin C · urea**

---

## The ideal marker — 3 criteria

1. **Freely filtered** at the glomerulus
2. Achieves a **stable plasma concentration**
3. **NOT reabsorbed, secreted or metabolized** by the kidney

---

## Why the two commonest markers fail

**CREATININE** — **secreted in the proximal tubule** (breaks criterion 3); concentration **influenced by age, sex, height and dietary protein**

> But **creatinine clearance is still the most widely used** — by **timed urine collection (24 or 6–12 hours)**

**UREA** — **reabsorbed by the renal tubules**; plasma level **affected by dietary protein and hydration**

---

## THE SCHWARTZ FORMULA — memorise it

> **GFR (mL/min/1.73 m²) = 0.413 × height (cm) ÷ serum creatinine (mg/dL)**

**Why 1.73 m²?** It is the **average body surface area of an ideal adult** — so results are comparable between a toddler and an adult.

---

# 4. TUBULAR FUNCTION

## FENa — the one to know

> **FENa (%) = (UNa × Pcr) / (PNa × Ucr) × 100**

| FENa | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **≤ 1** | **Normovolaemia and PRE-RENAL AKI** |
| **> 1** | **Impaired tubular transport, e.g. ATN** |

> **The kidney that is still working holds onto sodium (FENa ≤1). The damaged tubule leaks it (FENa >1).**

---

## Other tubular tests

- **Concentrating ability** — osmolality, pH
- **Water deprivation test** → distinguishes **central DI from nephrogenic DI**
- **Diluting ability test** → for **unexplained hyponatraemia**
- **Urine acidification test** → serial pH, bicarbonate excretion, titratable acid, NH₃ excretion, **ammonium chloride challenge**

---

# 5. IMAGING

## ULTRASOUND — the workhorse

**Sound waves above human hearing — > 20 kHz.** Transducer sends **and** receives; echoes → electrical impulses → image.

**Uses:** causes of UTI · causes of obstruction · **stones, masses, cysts, tumours** · congenital abnormality · **trauma** · transplant monitoring

| **MERITS** | **DEMERITS** |
|---|---|
| **No ionizing radiation** | **Cannot see through compact bone** (posterior acoustic shadow) |
| **Real time** | **Gas distorts image** |
| **Readily available** | **User dependent** |
| **Relatively cheap** | **Depth limitation** |
| **Portable, point of care** | |

---

## PLAIN FILM

![Plain film](img/plain-film-1.jpg)

For **nephrocalcinosis** · **radio-opaque stones** · congenital spinal abnormalities · **before contrast for IVU**

> **Declining with the advent of USS.**

---

## IVU — intravenous urography

![Intravenous urography](img/ivu.jpg)

**Shows anatomy** — details of **calyces and ureters**

**Diagnoses:** occult **duplex kidney**, **malrotation**, **site of obstruction**, **calculi** · can detect **renal scars**

**Demerits:** **high radiation** · low sensitivity for scars · contrast reaction · **NOT used in neonates, young infants, or renal failure**

---

## VCUG — voiding cysto-urethrography

![Voiding cysto-urethrography](img/vcug.jpg)

> **DEFINITIVE method for the LOWER urinary tract**
>
> **GOLD STANDARD for diagnosing and grading VUR and PUV obstruction**

Contrast injected **into the bladder**; films taken **during instillation, voiding and post-voiding**

**Indications:** suspected ureteric dilatation · small contracted kidneys · renal failure of indeterminate cause · **terminal haematuria** · voiding difficulties · **thick walled bladder on US**

**Demerits:** **high radiation dose · risk of UTI**

---

## RADIOISOTOPE STUDIES

**Dynamic renography** — **Tc-DTPA** and **Tc-MAG3**

**Uses:** **differential renal function** · indirect voiding cystography · post-transplant · evaluating surgery

**Low radiation dose.** **Demerits:** poor visualization of renal cortex; **cannot evaluate renal size or morphology**

---

**Static renal scan:** renovascular disease · dribbling incontinence · **ectopic/absent kidney** · **renal scars** · **acute pyelonephritis** (parenchymal involvement) · focal abnormalities

**Drawback:** time-consuming; **no information on function of the urinary tract**

---

## CT and MRI

**CT** — indications similar to USS · **clearer resolution** · **fine calcifications** · with contrast, **masses e.g. Wilms tumour** · **expensive** · **considerable radiation**

**MRI** — complex disorders · **excellent pelvic floor detail** · renal masses and cysts · **no radiation** · **heavy sedation needed**

---

# 6. RENAL BIOPSY

**Obtaining kidney tissue for histopathological evaluation.**

**Types:** **PERCUTANEOUS (preferred by paediatric nephrologists)** · transvenous/transjugular · laparoscopic · open surgical

![Renal biopsy needles](img/biopsy-needle-1.png)

**Needles:** Vim-Silverman · manual Tru-cut · semi-automatic Tru-cut · **Tru-cut spring-loaded automatic system**

---

## 6 general indications

1. **Exact diagnosis**
2. Determine **nature of therapy**
3. Decide **when treatment is futile**
4. Ascertain **active (reversible) vs chronic (irreversible)** changes
5. **Follow up**
6. **Research**

> **Biopsies with fewer than 5 glomeruli may not be accurate** — sample size matters, especially in focal lesions.

---

## When to biopsy — by condition

**NEPHROTIC SYNDROME:** age **< 1 or > 7 years** · **SRNS** · **evidence of nephritis** (hypertension, haematuria, low C3, or decreased renal function persisting despite volume correction)

**ACUTE GLOMERULONEPHRITIS:** course **not typical of PSAGN** · **HSP or SLE** · severity/therapy/prognosis · differentiate proliferative lesions (MPGN, C3 glomerulopathies, dense deposit disease) · **recurrent persistent haematuria or proteinuria**

**AKI:** **unexplained** AKI once pre- and post-renal excluded · AKI with nephritis, nephrotic syndrome, vasculitis or systemic disease · **persisting ATN** of uncertain cause · **suspected RPGN** (with anti-GBM, ANCA; immunofluorescence p-ANCA, c-ANCA, MPO, PR3)

**CKD:** **when kidneys are NOT shrunken** · primary disease · severity · **risk of recurrence after transplant** · donor suitability

**SYSTEMIC DISEASE:** SLE, atypical HUS · **diabetes with an atypical course**

**TRANSPLANT:** acute rejection · recurrence · **calcineurin toxicity** · infections · chronic allograft rejection

---

## When NOT to biopsy — 4 conditions

1. **Isolated glomerular haematuria (microscopic)**
2. **Isolated non-nephrotic haematuria**
3. **Childhood nephrotic syndrome aged > 1 and < 7 years**
4. **Frequently relapsing nephrotic syndrome**

---

## Transport media — a classic exam question

| Examination | Medium |
|---|---|
| **Light microscopy** | **10% buffered formalin** |
| **Immunofluorescence** | **Michel's transport media** |
| **Electron microscopy** | **GLUTARALDEHYDE** |

---

## CONTRAINDICATIONS

> **UNCORRECTABLE BLEEDING DIATHESIS = the ABSOLUTE contraindication**

Also: uncontrolled hypertension · active renal/peri-renal infection · **hydronephrosis** · obesity · ascites · **small shrunken kidneys** · tumours, large cysts, abscesses, pyelonephritis · **solitary, ectopic or horseshoe kidney** · uncooperative patient · **skin infection over the site** · no skilled operator or pathology support

---

## Pre-biopsy numbers — learn these

| Test | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **PCV** | **> 24–30%** |
| **Platelets** | **at least 100,000/mm³** |
| **INR** | **< 1.2** |
| **APTT** | **< 2× control or < 40 secs** |

**Also:** E&U and creatinine · **USS KUB to confirm TWO kidneys** · urinalysis · urine m/c/s · **group and cross-match 1 unit**

**And:** admit the day before · **no warfarin/anticoagulant/antithrombotic; no aspirin or NSAID for 7 days** · **control the blood pressure** · consent

**Haemodialysis patients:** wait **at least 24 hours** after the last session

**Prolonged bleeding time (> 8–10 min, e.g. SLE, azotaemia):** **IV desmopressin 0.3 µg/kg 30 min before**, or **intranasal DDAVP 2–4 µg/kg 2 hours before**

---

## Premedication

**NPO 4 hours** · commence **5% D/W** at maintenance · local anaesthesia and sedation

**Sedation:** **midazolam 0.1 mg/kg** (1–2 doses) + **ketamine 0.5–1.0 mg/kg** · ± **IV atropine 0.01 mg/kg** 1–2 minutes after midazolam

---

## The procedure

**Present:** paediatric nephrologist, radiologist, pathologist

**Position:** **PRONE for native kidney** · **SUPINE for graft kidney** · **lateral decubitus** if abdominal distension or ascites

**Monitor:** oxygen saturation, heart rate, blood pressure

> **The LEFT kidney is usually biopsied. The sample is taken from the LOWER POLE, from the CORTEX, under real-time ultrasound.**

Clean with **methylated spirit and povidone iodine** · **1% lignocaine** · nick the skin · advance under **real-time USS** · **16G vs 18G** · **2–3 passes** · **examine the core for glomeruli**

---

## Post-biopsy monitoring — the schedule

> **¼ hourly × 1 hour → half hourly × 2 hours → hourly × 4 hours → 4 hourly × 24 hours**

Keep a **urine rack** · **10% dextrose IV** at maintenance · input/output monitoring · eat when fully conscious

**Watch for:** **gross haematuria · abdominal pain · flank pain**

---

## 8 complications

**Significant haematuria · anaemia and hypotension · bowel or solid organ perforation/laceration · LOSS OF A KIDNEY · DEATH · AV malformation · hypertension · persistent loin pain**

---

# 7. BLADDER FUNCTION — URODYNAMIC STUDIES

Evaluate the **anatomy and physiology of the lower urinary tract** — especially **filling and emptying** of bladder and urethra.

**Useful in:** neurogenic bladder · urinary incontinence · voiding dysfunction · **VUR** · anorectal malformations

**NON-INVASIVE:** **uroflowmetry** (urine speed and volume) · **post-void residual urine**

**INVASIVE:** cystometry · pressure flow study · **electromyography**

---

## The fastest way to choose an imaging test

> **Structure, safe and cheap → ULTRASOUND**
>
> **Lower tract, VUR or PUV → VCUG (gold standard)**
>
> **Calyces and ureters → IVU**
>
> **Differential function → RADIOISOTOPE (DTPA/MAG3)**
>
> **Renal scars / acute pyelonephritis → STATIC SCAN**
>
> **Fine calcification or Wilms tumour → CT**
>
> **Complex anatomy, pelvic floor → MRI**
