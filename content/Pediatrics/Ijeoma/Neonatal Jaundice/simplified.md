# Neonatal Jaundice — Simplified

**In one line:** baby turns yellow because bilirubin builds up in the blood.

**Where bilirubin comes from:** old red blood cells break down → haem → bilirubin. Newborns make lots of it (their red cells die faster) and their liver is too immature to clear it fast. So it piles up.

**Two kinds:**
- **Normal (physiological):** shows up *after* day 1, peaks around day 3–5, gone by 2 weeks. No treatment usually.
- **Not normal (pathological):** shows up in the *first 24 hours*, OR climbs very fast, OR lasts *beyond 2 weeks*. Always hunt for a cause.

**Quick memory hook for timing:**
- Day 1 = danger (haemolysis, infection)
- Day 2–14 = usually normal
- After 14 days = check for biliary atresia and hypothyroidism

**How to check:** look in good light. Yellow spreads top-to-bottom (face → chest → legs). Yellow palms and soles = severe. Always confirm with a bilirubin measurement, not just your eyes.

**Why we worry — kernicterus:** if bilirubin gets very high it crosses into the brain and causes permanent damage (cerebral palsy, deafness). Preventing this is the whole point of treatment.

**Treatment:**
- **Phototherapy** (blue light) — first thing we do. Light changes bilirubin into a form the baby can pee/poo out.
- **Exchange transfusion** — for very high or dangerous levels.
- **Fix the cause** — antibiotics if infection, surgery if biliary atresia.
