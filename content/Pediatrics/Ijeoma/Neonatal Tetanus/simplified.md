# Neonatal Tetanus — Simplified

**One line:** a toxin from a soil bacterium gets into the cut cord, travels up the nerves, and switches off the brakes on every muscle.

---

## The organism

***Clostridium tetani***

- **Anaerobic** — only grows where there is no oxygen
- **Gram-positive bacillus**, motile, non-encapsulated
- Forms **spores** that survive heat, drying and disinfectants
- Spore sits at one end → looks like a **turkey leg / drumstick**
- Lives in **soil, dust, animal intestines, human faeces**

---

## How the baby gets it

**Umbilical cord contamination during unsanitary delivery** + **mother not immunised.**

Risk factors:

- Mother not immunised
- Unsterile cutting or care of the cord
- Foreign material on the stump — **animal dung**, ash, herbs

---

## Why the wound looks innocent

*C. tetani* **cannot provoke an inflammatory response.**

So the cord stump often looks **completely normal**.

> Do not wait for a nasty-looking stump. There may not be one.

---

## The two toxins

**Tetanolysin** — does nothing important. Ignore it.

**Tetanospasmin** — does everything.

- One of the **most potent toxins known**
- Lethal dose: **2.5 ng/kg**
- 150-kd protein = **100-kd heavy chain** + **50-kd light chain**, joined by a disulphide bond
- **Heavy chain** = binds to the presynaptic motor neuron
- **Light chain** = does the damage

---

## The mechanism, in five steps

1. Light chain enters the motor neuron
2. Travels **backwards up the axon** (retrograde axonal transport) to the spinal cord — takes **2–14 days**
3. Enters the **inhibitory** neurons
4. **Cleaves synaptobrevin** — the protein that lets vesicles dock and release
5. So **GABA and glycine are never released**

---

**GABA and glycine are the brakes.**

No brakes →

- Muscles contract uncontrollably (**spasms**)
- Autonomic system runs wild (**autonomic hyperactivity**)
- Ordinary noise, light or touch sets off a spasm

> **Tetanus is not too much accelerator. It is no brakes.**

---

## The cruellest detail

Once the toxin is **fixed to the neuron, antitoxin cannot touch it.**

Recovery needs **new nerve terminals to sprout and new synapses to form** — which takes weeks.

> That is why antitoxin only mops up toxin still circulating. Give it early or not at all.

---

## Four types

| Type | Key feature |
|---|---|
| **Generalised** | 3–14 days; trismus in 50–75%; opisthotonos |
| **Localised** | Spasm near the wound only; **mortality very low** |
| **Cephalic** | Rare; after head trauma or **chronic otitis media**; cranial nerve palsies |
| **Neonatal** | Cord contamination; **very poor prognosis** |

---

## What you see in the baby

Starts with feeding:

- **Poor sucking**, difficulty swallowing
- Then **rigidity and painful spasms**
- **Trismus** — lockjaw, from masseter spasm
- **Risus sardonicus** — the scornful smile, from facial muscle spasm
- **Opisthotonos** — arms flexed and adducted, fists clenched, legs extended
- Restless, irritable, **weak cry**
- Breathing trouble: fast breathing, apnoea, cyanosis, **laryngospasm**

---

> **The baby is fully awake and in severe pain during every spasm.** Sensorium is intact. That is why the dark quiet room is treatment, not comfort.

---

## Spasms are triggered by

**Noise · Light · Touch**

Which is exactly why: quiet, dark room, minimal handling.

---

## Complications

Aspiration pneumonia · Pneumothorax · Seizures · **Spinal fractures** · Pulmonary embolism · Gastric ulceration · Mediastinal emphysema

> The spasms are so violent they break bones and rupture tendons.

---

## Differential diagnosis

- Acute encephalitis · Meningitis
- Epileptic seizures
- Parapharyngeal / retropharyngeal abscess
- Rabies

**Other causes of trismus:** alveolar abscess, **strychnine poisoning**, dystonic drug reactions, **hypocalcaemic tetany**

**The distinguishing sign:**

> Increased tone in **central** muscles + generalised spasms + **relative sparing of hands and feet** = tetanus.

---

## Investigations

> **Diagnosis is clinical. There is NO confirmatory test.**

Others, for support and complications: leukocytosis (secondary wound infection), CSF, EEG, blood culture, random blood glucose.

---

## Treatment — six pillars

1. **Supportive** — IV fluids, oxygen, **quiet dark room**
2. **Antibiotics** — kill the source
3. **Antitoxin** — neutralise circulating toxin
4. **Control spasms** — diazepam
5. **Respiratory care** — gentle suctioning, tracheostomy
6. **Manage complications**

Plus: **rigorously cleanse the umbilical stump** — stop toxin production at source.

---

### Antibiotics

**Amoxicillin-clavulanate** 20–30 mg/kg/day, 8 hourly × 7 days **AND metronidazole**

Or **ceftriaxone** 50 mg/kg 12 hourly × 7 days, or **cefotaxime** 50 mg/kg 6 hourly × 5 days

---

### Antitoxin

**TIG 250–500 units IM**, single dose.

If unavailable → **TAT 1500 IU IM** (test for horse serum sensitivity first).

Plus a **single dose of DT vaccine**.

---

### Spasm control — three drugs, rotated

- **Diazepam** 0.1–0.2 mg/kg every 3–6 h IV, titrated
- **Chlorpromazine** 1–5 mg/kg/dose every 8 h
- **Phenobarbitone** loading 20 mg/kg, then 2.5 mg/kg 12-hourly, up to 5 mg/kg

Rotation: **Diazepam** 0,3,9,15,21,24 · **Chlorpromazine** 3,9,15,21 · **Phenobarbitone** 0,6,21

---

### Magnesium sulphate — for autonomic instability

- Loading **5 g (or 75 mg/kg)** IV
- Then **2.3 g/h** infusion until spasms controlled
- **Monitor the patellar reflex.** Areflexia = top of therapeutic range (**4 mmol/L**) → reduce the dose

---

## Prevention — the real answer

> **Tetanus is completely preventable by active immunization.**

- **At least 2 doses of tetanus toxoid** to the mother and other pregnant women in the locality
- Supplemental immunization for **women of childbearing age**
- Improve routine coverage and maternal immunization
- **Train birth attendants** — clean cord cutting and care
- **TT boosters at 10 years**
- Sterile instrument for cutting the cord
- Clean wounds promptly with **hydrogen peroxide**

---

## Prognosis

**Mortality about 90%.**

**Four bad prognostic signs:**

1. Onset in the **first week** of life
2. Trismus-to-spasm interval **under 48 hours**
3. High fever
4. Tachycardia

> And the sting: **surviving tetanus gives you no immunity.** He must still be actively immunised after recovery.
