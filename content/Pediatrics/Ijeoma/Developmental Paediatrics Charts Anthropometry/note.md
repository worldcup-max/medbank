## Growth vs development

- **Growth** = the **increase in SIZE** of a child
- **Development** = the **progressive acquisition of skills** — physical (motor), cognitive (thought), linguistic (communication) and social (emotional)

> Development is an **increase in function and complexity of skills**, whereas growth is an increase in size.

## Factors affecting growth and development

- **Biologic** — genetic inheritance, intrauterine infection or teratogens, perinatal accidents, neurologic and hormonal maturation
- **Psychological** — the mother figure, feeding practices, child-rearing environment
- **Social** — family socio-economic status, extended family, cultural practices, geo-political milieu

## Phases of childhood

Prenatal · Neonatal · Infant · Preschool · School age · Adolescence

## Patterns of growth

**Neonatal** (first 28 days): usually weighs **≥ 2,500 g**, length **> 45 cm**. Weight may **drop 3–10% in the first week** (loss of extravascular fluid, low colostrum intake).

**Infant** (first 12 months):

- **Weight** gain ~30 g/day (2nd month), slowing to 20 g/day (3rd–5th). Rule of thumb: an exclusively breastfed term baby **doubles birth weight by ~5 months and triples it by 9–12 months**
- **Length** — highest velocity: from **≥ 45 cm at birth to ≥ 66 cm at 1 year** (~2 cm/month average)
- **Head circumference** — parallels the brain's growth: from **33 cm at birth to 45 cm at 1 year**

**Preschool (2–5 yr):** velocities ~240 g/month, 1 cm/month, 0.25 cm/month (weight, height, HC).

**Primary school (6–12 yr):** ~3 kg/year, 6 cm/year, 0.5 cm/year.

**Adolescence (13–18 yr, may span 10–20):** three phases — early (10–13), middle (14–16), late (17–20).

## Formulas (weight & height up to 12 years)

| Age | Weight (kg) | Height (cm) |
|---|---|---|
| **Birth** | 3 | 48 |
| **3–12 months** | (age in months + 9) ÷ 2 | — |
| **1–6 years** | (age in years × 2) + 8 | (age × 6) + 77 |
| **7–12 years** | (age in years × 7 − 5) ÷ 2 | (age × 6) + 77 |

## Fields of development

**Gross motor · Vision and fine motor · Hearing, speech and language (cognitive) · Social, emotional and behavioural (psychological) · Vegetative.**

## Screening vs assessment

- **Screening** — checks of a **whole population/groups** at set ages by trained professionals
- **Assessment** — **detailed analysis** of overall or specific development

**Global developmental delay** = delay in **2 or more spheres in a child < 5 years**. After 5 years the term **mental retardation** (delayed IQ) is used.

## Growth assessment & monitoring

Parameters: **weight, height, head circumference, mid-arm circumference, skinfold thickness, body proportions, skeletal maturation, dental development**.

**Growth monitoring** uses the **growth chart** (an international NCHS reference standard) to track **weight-for-age, height-for-age, HC-for-age and weight-for-height** from birth. Physiologic shifts across curves occur with **parental genetics (first 2 years)**, **prematurity** (apply gestational correction until **18 months for HC, 24 months for weight, 40 months for height**), and the **adolescent growth spurt**.

## Reading the growth chart (deviation from the norm)

- **Failure to thrive:** weight **below the 5th percentile** or **drops below 2 major percentile lines**
- **Acute undernutrition:** **weight-for-age and weight-for-height drop**, but **height-for-age stays normal**
- **Stunting:** **height-for-age drops**, while weight-for-height may return toward normal
- In **nutritional** causes, **weight drops before or with height**; in **constitutional, familial and endocrine** causes, the **height curve is affected first**
- **Obesity:** weight-for-height **> 120%** of median, with raised **BMI**

## Body proportions

The body is divided at the **symphysis pubis**: upper segment (pubis→crown) and lower (pubis→sole). The **upper:lower (U/L) ratio** is **~1.7 at birth, 1.3 at 3 years, 1.0 after 7 years**.

- **Higher** ratios → short-limb dwarfism, rickets
- **Lower** ratios → some adolescents, sickle cell disease

## Dentition

Mineralisation begins in the **2nd trimester**; deciduous teeth complete by ~3 years, permanent by ~25 years.

- **Delayed eruption** = no first tooth by **13 months** (familial, idiopathic, **hypothyroidism, rickets**)
- **Early exfoliation** (before the 5th birthday) — idiopathic, trauma, gingivitis, **scurvy, histiocytosis X**

## Developmental domains and pattern

Domains: **gross motor, fine motor, social, emotional, communication, cognition.**

When assessing a young child: consider the **age**, focus questions on the area of **likely maximum progress**, offer **suitable toys**, and **observe** how the child uses them and interacts. Instruments: **Denver II**, Bayley scales, PEDS, Paediatric Symptom Checklist, **M-CHAT** (autism screen).

**Who assesses and why:** parents (reassurance/detection), teachers (school performance), physicians (identify specific delay/disability and plan intervention).

## Recognising speech and language problems early

Refer if: **not talking at all by 2 years**; speech **largely unintelligible after 3 years**; dropping beginning consonants after 3 years; **not using 2–3-word sentences by 3 years**; mostly vowel sounds; word endings missing after 5 years; or a **monotone/too-loud/too-soft/poor-quality voice** (may indicate hearing loss).

## The Draw-a-Person test

A standardised way to assess **intellectual and emotional state** — first used by **Florence Goodenough (1926)**, validated for **Nigerian children aged 6–11** by Izuora and Ebigbo. Done anywhere with pencil and paper.

- **4 points = 1 "draw-a-person" year**; no points expected before age 4, so **3 points are added**
- **Maturation age = (points ÷ 4) + 3**
- **Man-drawing quotient = maturation age ÷ chronologic age**; normal **> 0.75** (i.e. **> 75% = IQ normal**)
