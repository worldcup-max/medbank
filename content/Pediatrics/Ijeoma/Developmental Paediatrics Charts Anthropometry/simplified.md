# Developmental Paediatrics — the simple version

## Two words that sound alike but aren't

> **Growth = getting BIGGER (size). Development = getting BETTER (skills).**

A child *grows* (weight, height, head) and *develops* (learns to sit, talk, socialise). Keep them separate — the whole topic splits along this line: **anthropometry** measures growth; **milestones** measure development.

---

## What shapes a child — 3 buckets

> **Biologic** (genes, infections, birth injury, hormones) · **Psychological** (mother, feeding, home) · **Social** (money, family, culture).

---

## Growth numbers you actually need

Don't drown in the velocities. Anchor on the **milestone weights and lengths:**

**Weight:**
> **Doubles birth weight by ~5 months. Triples it by ~1 year.**

**Length:**
> **~50 cm at birth → ~75 cm at 1 year** (roughly +25 cm in year one). Head grows **33 → 45 cm** in the first year.

**The first-week dip (don't panic):**
> A newborn can **lose 3–10% of weight in the first week** — normal (fluid loss + low colostrum). It's regained by ~2 weeks.

---

## The formulas — learn these, they're free marks

For a child whose exact weight you don't have:

> **1–6 years: weight (kg) = (age × 2) + 8**
> **Height (1–12 yr): (age × 6) + 77**

So a 4-year-old ≈ (4×2)+8 = **16 kg**, and ≈ (4×6)+77 = **101 cm**. Quick, examinable, and useful in emergencies (drug doses).

---

## The growth chart — the single most important skill

The chart plots the child against percentiles. Learn to read **four patterns** — this is where exams live:

> **Failure to thrive** → weight **< 5th percentile** OR **crosses down 2 major lines.**
>
> **Acute undernutrition (wasting)** → **weight drops, HEIGHT stays normal** (recent problem — no time to affect height yet).
>
> **Stunting (chronic)** → **HEIGHT drops** (long-standing problem has stunted growth).

**The golden rule that tells you the CAUSE:**

> **Nutritional cause → WEIGHT drops first.**
> **Endocrine / genetic / constitutional cause → HEIGHT drops first.**

Think about why: starve a child and they get **thin** before they get **short**. But a hormone problem (e.g. growth hormone, hypothyroid) stunts **height** while weight may be fine — so a **short but chubby** child suggests an endocrine cause.

---

## Body proportions — the U/L ratio

Divide the body at the pubis: upper half and lower half.

> **U/L ratio: 1.7 at birth → 1.3 at 3 years → 1.0 after 7 years.**

The legs "catch up" as the child grows, so the ratio falls toward 1.

- **High ratio (short legs)** → **rickets, short-limb dwarfism**
- **Low ratio (long legs)** → **sickle cell disease**, some adolescents

---

## Teeth — two numbers

> **No first tooth by 13 months = delayed eruption** → think **hypothyroidism, rickets** (or just familial).
>
> **Teeth falling out before age 5 = early exfoliation** → think **scurvy, histiocytosis X**, trauma.

---

## Development — the fields and the delay definition

Development is tracked in **domains: gross motor, fine motor, speech/language, social, cognition.**

> **Global developmental delay = delayed in 2+ areas, in a child UNDER 5.**
> After 5, the term becomes **mental retardation (low IQ).**

**Speech red flags (refer!):**
> **Not talking at all by 2 · unintelligible after 3 · no 2–3 word sentences by 3 · monotone/odd voice (? hearing loss).**

---

## The Draw-a-Person test — the one calculation

A cheap IQ/emotional screen (pencil + paper), validated for **Nigerian children 6–11**.

> **Maturation age = (points ÷ 4) + 3**
> **Quotient = maturation age ÷ chronological age; normal > 0.75 (> 75%).**

The "+3" exists because kids can't score until age 4 — so you add the years they couldn't be tested.

---

## Putting it all together

**Growth = size; development = skills.** For growth, anchor the milestones (**double weight at 5 months, triple at 1 year; head 33→45 cm**), the **weight/height formulas** ((age×2)+8; (age×6)+77), and — most importantly — **reading the growth chart**: FTT = under 5th percentile or crossing 2 lines; **weight-first drop = nutritional, height-first drop = endocrine/genetic.**

For development: track the **domains**, know **global delay = 2+ areas under age 5**, the **speech red flags**, and the **Draw-a-Person** quotient (**> 0.75 = normal**). And two body clues: **U/L ratio (rickets high, sickle cell low)** and **teeth (late eruption = hypothyroid/rickets; early loss = scurvy).**
