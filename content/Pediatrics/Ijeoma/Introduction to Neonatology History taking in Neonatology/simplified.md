# Neonatology & the Newborn — the simple version

## Two time windows to never mix up

> **Neonatal = the first 28 days of LIFE.**
> **Perinatal = 20–28 weeks of pregnancy → the first 7 days of life.**

Perinatal *straddles birth* — that's why it catches stillbirths plus early newborn deaths. Neonatal starts the moment the baby is out.

---

## Why the newborn is fragile

> At birth the placenta is cut off, so the baby must suddenly do everything itself — **breathe, keep warm, control sugar, clear bilirubin** — all at once.

A small failure in any of these kills fast. That's the whole reason neonatology exists.

---

## The mortality numbers (UNICEF 2020)

> **IMR 72** (first year) · **PMR 41** (stillbirth + first week) · **NMR 35** (first 28 days), all per 1000.

The one insight that matters:

> **NMR (35) ≈ half of IMR (72).** Half of all deaths in a baby's first *year* happen in its first *month.* Fix the newborn period and you halve infant mortality.

---

## What actually kills newborns — the big four

> **Prematurity/low birth weight · Birth asphyxia · Infection (sepsis) · Congenital malformation.**

And a trick for the categories:

> **Preterm deaths = an immature organ fails** — lungs→RDS, brain→IVH, gut→NEC.
> **Term deaths = the delivery or anatomy** — asphyxia, trauma, meconium, malformation.

Most are **preventable** — in the antenatal clinic and labour ward, not the nursery.

---

## The newborn history is really the PREGNANCY history

> A newborn has no past of its own — so its "past medical history" lives inside the **pregnancy, labour and delivery** story.

Walk the timeline: *pregnancy* (planned? illness/drugs/bleeding) → *labour* (spontaneous or induced? prolonged? membranes) → *delivery* (normal or forceps/vacuum?) → *first minutes* (did it cry, or need resuscitation?) → feeding → immunisation.

Don't skip the **family reproductive history** — a previous **rhesus/blood-group incompatibility** can produce a very sick baby.

---

## Examining the newborn — the numbers

> **Weight 2.5–3.5 kg · Length 45–55 cm · Head (OFC) 35 ± 2 cm · posture flexed with clenched fists.**

Two swellings students confuse on the head:

> **Caput = soft, CROSSES suture lines. Cephalhaematoma = does NOT cross sutures.**

Normal-but-scary-looking skin findings (all benign): **acrocyanosis, lanugo, vernix, milia, Mongolian blue spots.**

One red flag pairing:

> **Scaphoid (sunken) abdomen + respiratory distress → diaphragmatic hernia** (gut has gone up into the chest).

And: **no meconium by 48 hours → look for obstruction** (check the anus is patent).

---

## APGAR — the mnemonic IS the score

> **A**ppearance · **P**ulse · **G**rimace · **A**ctivity · **R**espiration — each 0/1/2, max 10.

Two cut-offs to lock: **Pulse <100 = 1, >100 = 2**; **colour "1" = pink body, blue hands/feet.**

> **0–3 severe · 4–6 moderate · 7+ vigorous.** (You don't wait for the score to start resuscitating — it grades severity and tracks response at 1 and 5 min.)

---

## Resuscitation — let the heart rate decide

> **HR above 60 → ventilate** (bag & mask + O₂). The heart is fine; the problem is breathing.
> **HR below 60 → ventilate + chest compressions + adrenaline.** Now the heart is failing too.

The heart rate is the dial that tells you how aggressive to be.

---

## Routine care every newborn gets

> **Eyes:** silver nitrate (Credé's) — prevents birth-canal eye infection.
> **Cord:** chlorhexidine, daily until it drops off.
> **Vitamin K 0.5 mg IM stat** — prevents bleeding (newborns are K-deficient).
> **Breast within 30 minutes** — no water/glucose first (prelacteal feeds are wrong).

---

## Putting it together

**Neonatal = first 28 days; perinatal straddles birth.** The newborn is fragile because it suddenly takes over the placenta's jobs. Half of infant deaths are neonatal, and the killers — **prematurity, asphyxia, infection, malformation** — are mostly preventable. The **history is the pregnancy story**; the **exam** has fixed normals (2.5–3.5 kg, 45–55 cm, OFC 35±2) and a few red flags (scaphoid abdomen, no meconium by 48 h). **APGAR** grades severity (0–3/4–6/7+), and **resuscitation follows the heart rate** (>60 ventilate, <60 add compressions + adrenaline). Finish with routine care: **eyes, cord, Vitamin K, breast within 30 minutes.**
